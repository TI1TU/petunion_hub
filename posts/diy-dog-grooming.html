DIY Dog Grooming: How to Keep Your Pet Clean at Home

Mar 25, 2026 By PetUnion Team 8 min read

The Benefits of At-Home Grooming

Taking your dog to a professional groomer every four to six weeks can quickly become incredibly expensive. While professional grooms are necessary for certain complex cuts (like Poodle clips or stripping terriers), learning basic DIY grooming at home can save you hundreds of dollars while simultaneously providing a wonderful bonding opportunity with your pet.

Grooming is not just about keeping your dog looking pretty—it is an essential aspect of their overall health. Regular brushing prevents painful matting, bathing prevents serious skin infections, and nail trimming keeps their joints properly aligned. Here are our top grooming tips for doing it yourself.

1. Brushing: The Foundation of Grooming

Brushing is the most critical step in the grooming process. It removes loose fur, distributes natural skin oils, and removes dirt before it becomes impacted in their coat.

  • Short-Haired Breeds (Pugs, Beagles, Dobermans): Need brushing once a week with a rubber curry brush or a soft bristle brush.
  • Medium to Long-Haired Breeds (Golden Retrievers, Collies): Require brushing several times a week with a slicker brush to prevent painful undercoat matting.
  • Curly-Haired Breeds (Poodles, Doodles): Require daily, deeply thorough brushing using a slicker brush and a metal greyhound comb to ensure mats are not forming near the skin.
Pro Tip: Never cut mats out with scissors! It is incredibly easy to accidentally cut your dog's skin, as mats pull the skin upward. Use a mat splitter or clippers, or take them to a professional if heavily matted.

2. Bath Time Doesn't Have to Be a Battle

Most dogs only need a bath once a month. Over-bathing strips the coat of natural, protective oils and leads to severely dry, itchy skin.

  • Preparation: Thoroughly brush your dog *before* the bath. Putting water on a matted coat acts like cement, tightening the mats and making them impossible to brush out later.
  • Use the Right Shampoo: Never use human shampoo! A dog's skin pH is vastly different from ours. Choose an oatmeal-based, hypoallergenic dog shampoo.
  • Water Temperature: Use lukewarm water. Hot water can overheat and stress your dog, while freezing water is miserable.
  • Drying: Towel dry them as much as possible. If using a hair dryer, use the lowest heat setting to prevent terrible burns. High-velocity pet dryers (which use room temperature air to blow water off) are a fantastic investment for fluffy dogs.

3. Trimming the Nails

Long nails can cause a dog's toes to splay out, leading to tremendous pain and long-term arthritis. If you hear a "click-click-click" when they walk on hardwood floors, their nails are too long.

Nail trimming at home requires extreme patience. You only want to cut off the sharp tip, carefully avoiding the "quick"—the pink blood vessel running through the center of the nail. If you accidentally cut the quick, it will bleed profusely. Always have styptic powder on hand to stop bleeding instantly.

If clippers are too terrifying for you or the dog, consider purchasing an electric pet nail grinder (a Dremel) to slowly file the nails down instead. It is much safer and softer.

4. Cleaning the Ears and Teeth

Do not forget the extremities! Ears should be wiped out weekly using a dog-safe ear-cleaning solution and a cotton ball (never use a Q-Tip down the ear canal, as it can completely rupture their eardrum).

Pet health also depends heavily on dental hygiene. Daily brushing with a dog-specific enzymatic toothpaste (human toothpaste contains xylitol, which is lethally toxic to dogs) will prevent dental disease and save you thousands in future veterinary dental extraction bills.

Conclusion

With the right tools, plenty of high-value treats (like a lick mat smeared with peanut butter), and a calm demeanor, DIY grooming can become an enjoyable, stress-free routine rather than an anxiety-inducing chore. Start slow, generously reward cooperation, and enjoy keeping your furry friend happy, clean, and healthy!
