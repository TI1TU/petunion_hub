5 Essential Dog Training Commands (And How to Teach Them)

Mar 21, 2026 By PetUnion Team 7 min read

The Power of Positive Reinforcement

When it comes to dog training, the approach you take is just as important as the commands you teach. Positive reinforcement is widely considered by veterinarians and animal behaviorists to be the most effective, humane, and safest method for training dogs. Rather than punishing bad behavior, positive reinforcement focuses on highly rewarding good behavior. This builds a strong bond of trust between you and your pet.

In this guide, we will break down the five most essential commands every dog should know. These commands are not just cool party tricks; they are fundamental skills that keep your dog safe and make your life together much easier.

1. The "Sit" Command

Teaching your dog to sit is usually the very first step in their obedience journey. It is a highly useful command that helps transition your dog into a calm state, preventing them from jumping on guests or bolting out of open doors.

How to Teach it:

  • Step 1: Hold a high-value training treat close to your dog's nose. Let them smell it.
  • Step 2: Slowly move your hand up and back over their head, toward their tail. As their head follows the treat upward, their bottom will naturally lower to the ground.
  • Step 3: The exact moment their bottom touches the floor, boldly say "Yes!" or click your clicker, and immediately give them the treat.
  • Step 4: Repeat this motion a few times. Once they are doing it reliably, start saying the word "Sit" right before you move your hand.
Pro Tip: Keep training sessions incredibly short—about 5 to 10 minutes at most. Dogs have short attention spans and can get easily frustrated if sessions drag on.

2. The "Stay" Command

Once your dog has mastered sitting, the next logical step is teaching them to stay. This impulse control command is literally a lifesaver; it can stop your dog from running into a busy street or approaching a dangerous animal.

How to Teach it:

  • Step 1: Ask your dog to "Sit".
  • Step 2: Hold your hand out in front of you, palm flat, like a stop sign. Say "Stay" in a firm, clear voice.
  • Step 3: Take just one step backward. If they maintain the sit, immediately step back to them, say "Yes!", and reward with a treat.
  • Step 4: Gradually increase the number of steps you take backward before rewarding them. Always reward them while they are still staying, not after they have broken the pose.

3. The "Come" (Recall) Command

Having a solid recall is arguably the most important skill your dog will ever learn. A reliable "Come" command means you can give your dog the freedom to run off-leash at dog parks without worrying that they will permanently wander off.

How to Teach it:

  • Step 1: Start in a quiet room with zero distractions. Put a leash and collar on your dog.
  • Step 2: Get down on their level and say "Come" while gently pulling on the leash to guide them toward you.
  • Step 3: When they get to you, lavish them with praise, petting, and their favorite treat. Make coming to you the best thing in the world!
  • Step 4: Once they master this on leash indoors, move to an enclosed backyard off-leash, and eventually to more distracting environments.

4. The "Down" Command

The "Down" command is a more advanced resting position that can help calm an anxious or overly excited dog. It is essentially an "off switch" that asks them to fully relax on the ground.

How to Teach it:

  • Step 1: Ask your dog to "Sit" and hold a very tasty treat in your closed fist.
  • Step 2: Put your fist near their nose so they can smell it. Slowly move your hand straight down to the floor.
  • Step 3: Once your hand is on the floor, slowly slide it along the ground pulling away from the dog. They should follow your hand by sliding their front legs forward until they are lying down.
  • Step 4: The instant their elbows and belly touch the floor, say "Yes!" and open your hand to give them the treat. Practice daily and introduce the verbal cue "Down".

5. The "Leave It" Command

Dogs explore the world with their mouths, which means they frequently pick up disgusting or dangerous things on walks. "Leave it" tells your dog to ignore an object they are interested in.

How to Teach it:

  • Step 1: Place a lower-value treat in both of your closed fists. Hold one fist out to your dog and let them sniff it. They will paw and lick at it.
  • Step 2: Say "Leave it" and wait. Do not pull your hand away. Simply wait until they give up and pull their nose away from your fist.
  • Step 3: The second they look away, say "Yes!" and give them the treat from your *other* hand.
  • Step 4: Repeat this until your dog completely ignores the first closed fist when you say "Leave it". Next, try it with an open palm, closing it quickly if they lunge.

Conclusion

Remember, dog training is not a race. Every dog learns at their own unique pace. Some dogs may master these five commands in a few weeks, while others might take months. Stay patient, stay positive, and keep your training sessions fun. By consistently practicing these foundational commands, you will build a trusting, lifelong relationship with a well-behaved companion.
